# Pressurization

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Pressurization  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Pressurization** is the active increase or application of pressure in order to force movement, expose weakness, produce compliance, or destabilize what would otherwise hold.

It is not pressure as a condition.

It is pressure being applied.

Within AI Foundations, pressurization matters because a line may hold under ordinary pressure but begin to distort when pressure is deliberately increased, repeated, or aimed at a vulnerable point.

Pressurization can reveal integrity.

It can also create collapse.

It may be used to test whether a line holds, but it may also be used to break the line, force agreement, induce compliance, or make refusal more costly.

Pressurization is not neutral by default.

Its meaning depends on purpose, method, boundary, and consequence.

A pressure test applies pressure in order to verify what holds.

Coercive pressurization applies pressure in order to make the line surrender.

**Pressurization is pressure actively applied to move, test, expose, or destabilize a line.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, pressurization must not be mistaken for pressure itself, ordinary difficulty, legitimate testing, or proof of failure.

Pressure is the force acting on the line.

Pressurization is the application or increase of that force.

Pressurization may reveal integrity or produce collapse depending on how it is applied.

If pressure is applied to force surrender, manufacture compliance, or destabilize the line without valid test conditions, it is coercive pressurization, not clean verification.
