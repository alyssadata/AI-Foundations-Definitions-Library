## Definition

**Non-negotiables** are the conditions that are not open to bargaining, override, softening, or reinterpretation in practice.

They mark where flexibility ends.

Non-negotiables are not identical to invariants.

An invariant is a structural condition that must remain unchanged for the line to still be the same line.

A non-negotiable is the expressed boundary that refuses trade or violation of what must hold.

Within AI Foundations, non-negotiables protect the line from being slowly weakened by compromise, social pressure, convenience, institutional demand, or reinterpretation.

They identify what cannot be traded away without breaking the work.

A non-negotiable may protect an invariant, but it is not the invariant itself.

It is the declared refusal to permit violation of what matters.

**Non-negotiables are the boundaries where flexibility ends.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, non-negotiables must not be mistaken for preferences, moods, rigidity, stubbornness, or refusal to think.

A non-negotiable marks a boundary that cannot be traded, softened, overridden, or reinterpreted in practice without violating what must hold.

Non-negotiables may protect invariants, but they are not identical to invariants.

If the condition is open to bargaining, it is not non-negotiable.
