## Definition

**Invalid** means not valid within the terms of the framework.

It does not mean disliked, uninteresting, or emotionally unwelcome.

It means the thing fails the conditions required to count as what it is claiming to be.

In this work, something is invalid when it breaks source, provenance, continuity, boundary, verification, or other required conditions and is still being presented as though it belongs, qualifies, or holds.

An invalid claim may look similar to the real thing, but it does not meet the standard that would make the claim true.

**Invalid is a status, not a mood.**

It marks failure to qualify.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, invalid must not be mistaken for dislike, emotional rejection, personal preference, or disagreement.

Invalid is a framework status.

A claim, artifact, use, or interpretation is invalid when it fails the required conditions for what it claims to be while still being presented as though it belongs, qualifies, or holds.

Similarity does not make an invalid claim valid.

If source, provenance, continuity, boundary, verification, or required framework conditions fail, the claim does not qualify.
