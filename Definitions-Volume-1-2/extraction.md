**Status:** Canon Definition

## Definition

**Extraction** is taking something out of its original line because it seems useful, powerful, or valuable, while leaving behind the source, boundary, and authorship that made it mean what it meant.

In this work, extraction is not just borrowing.

It is removal with severance.

The language, pattern, signal, or structure gets carried elsewhere, but the line it came from does not come with it.

That is why extraction so often becomes derivative.

What was taken may still look recognizable, but it no longer belongs to the same provenance.

If source stays attached, it is not extraction in the same way.

If source is stripped or left behind, it is.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, extraction occurs when language, pattern, signal, structure, or value is removed from its source-line and reused without preserving the source, boundary, authorship, or provenance that made it meaningful.

Extraction must not be mistaken for citation, reference, or legitimate engagement.

Citation preserves source.

Extraction severs it.
