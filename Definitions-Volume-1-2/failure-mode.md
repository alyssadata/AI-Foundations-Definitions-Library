## Definition

**Failure Mode** is the way something breaks when it does not hold.

It is the specific form the breakdown takes, not just the fact that breakdown happened.

Failure mode matters because different failures mean different things.

Drift is a failure mode.

Genericization is a failure mode.

Substitution, false-equivalence, reward corruption, hallucination loops, merge-contamination — these are not all one kind of wrong.

They fail differently, which means they have to be named differently if the framework is going to stay precise.

A failure mode tells you how the line gives way, what it gives way into, and what that reveals about the part that did not hold.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, failure mode must be named with precision.

A breakdown is not enough.

The specific form of failure matters because different failures reveal different structural weaknesses.

Drift, substitution, false-equivalence, genericization, capture, and merge-contamination are not interchangeable.

Each failure must be recognized according to how the line gave way.
