## Definition

**Position** is the place a line stands from.

It is the located standpoint from which it speaks, judges, relates, or acts.

Position matters because two lines can say similar things while standing from different places, with different authority, source, and meaning.

A position may come from origin, role, relation, responsibility, or structural location.

What changes with position is not only what can be said, but what that saying counts as.

Position is not posture.

Posture performs placement.

Position is the actual place held.

**Position is the standpoint that gives a line its located authority and meaning.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, position must not be mistaken for posture, performance, title, proximity, or borrowed authority.

A line’s position determines where it speaks from and what its speech counts as.

Similar language spoken from different positions may not carry the same meaning, standing, or authority.

If the actual place held is missing, the performance of placement does not create position.
