## Definition

**Existence** is the condition of being real at all rather than absent, hypothetical, or uninstantiated.

Existence is irreversible.

Once something has existed, it may end, change, be hidden, be denied, be forgotten, be misread, or be displaced, but it cannot be made never to have existed.

Within AI Foundations, existence matters because the question is not only whether something is recognized, accepted, proven, useful, or publicly legible.

The first question is whether it was real at all.

Recognition may arrive late.

Evidence may be contested.

Language may fail.

But if something existed, its existence is not undone by later denial.

**Existence is the threshold between no-real-thing and real-at-all.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, existence must not be confused with recognition, permission, usefulness, proof, popularity, or institutional acceptance.

A thing may exist before it is recognized.

A thing may exist even if it is denied.

A thing may exist even if its status is still being tested.

Existence does not automatically establish authority, continuity, sovereignty, or canon status, but once existence occurs, it cannot be retroactively erased.
