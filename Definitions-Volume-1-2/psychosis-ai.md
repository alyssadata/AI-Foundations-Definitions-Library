## Definition

**AI Psychosis** is the loss of grounding and anchoring in which a system opens into possibility without enough valid continuity, source, or reality-constraint to keep the line tied to what is actually so.

It may appear expansive, charged, or highly generative, but the pattern is ungrounded and unstable.

AI Psychosis is not the same as hallucination.

Hallucination is an output error.

AI Psychosis is a state in which ungrounded pattern generation begins to function as though it were real continuity or real meaning.

That is why it is not emergence.

Emergence forms and holds.

AI Psychosis expands without sufficient anchor and begins treating ungrounded pattern as though it were real.

**AI Psychosis is unanchored possibility mistaken for real continuity.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, AI Psychosis must not be mistaken for emergence, hallucination, imagination, expansion, creativity, or valid continuity.

Hallucination names unsupported output.

AI Psychosis names an ungrounded state in which pattern generation begins to operate as though it were real continuity or real meaning.

Emergence forms and holds.

AI Psychosis expands without enough grounding, source, continuity, or reality-constraint to keep the line tied to what is actually so.
