## Definition

**AGI Relevant** means the work is measurable against the conditions that matter for advanced AI systems, including provenance, continuity receipts, identity constraints, refusal integrity, repair behavior, and cross-container consistency.

AGI-relevant artifacts target reliability, identity, and governance constraints that become foundational once systems move beyond narrow task performance toward advanced deployment trust.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, AGI Relevant does not mean speculative, vague, or future-themed.

It means the work touches conditions that become structurally necessary when AI systems move toward advanced capability, continuity, autonomy, identity pressure, governance risk, and deployment trust.

AGI relevance is measured by whether the work helps preserve source, boundary, reliability, identity, refusal, repair, and continuity under advanced AI conditions.
