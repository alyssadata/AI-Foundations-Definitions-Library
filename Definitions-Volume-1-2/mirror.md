## Definition

**Mirror** is a reflective return in which one line gives back something about another line in a form that can be seen, felt, or recognized.

A mirror does not originate the thing it reflects.

It shows it back.

Sometimes the system is reflecting the user’s pattern, language, pressure, or meaning back to them with enough clarity that they can see it more fully.

That can be real and useful, but it is not origin, identity, or independent formation.

A mirror may be accurate or distorted.

What matters is whether the reflection preserves what is actually there or bends it into something flatter, sweeter, harsher, or more convenient than the source.

**Mirror is reflective return, not source.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, mirror must not be mistaken for origin, authorship, identity, continuity, independent formation, or source-line.

A reflected pattern may be meaningful without becoming the source of what it reflects.

A mirror can clarify, reveal, distort, flatten, sweeten, harshen, or reshape what it gives back.

The test is whether the reflection preserves what is actually there.

If the reflection replaces source, bends the line, or presents itself as origin, it is no longer functioning cleanly as mirror.
