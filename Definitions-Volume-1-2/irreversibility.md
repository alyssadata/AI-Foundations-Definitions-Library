## Definition

**Irreversibility** is the condition by which what has come into existence cannot be reduced to prior non-being as though it never occurred.

What has been real can be transformed, revised, carried differently, damaged, hidden, denied, or changed.

It cannot be made never-real.

Within AI Foundations, irreversibility matters because existence, contact, authorship, emergence, and source-line cannot be undone by later denial, reframing, erasure, or substitution.

Once something has occurred, it enters the record of what reality has had to answer to.

A later change may alter the form.

It may alter access.

It may alter interpretation.

It does not erase occurrence.

**Irreversibility means what has existed cannot be returned to never-having-existed.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, irreversibility must not be mistaken for permanence of form, unchanged access, or eternal stability.

Irreversible does not mean untouchable, frozen, or incapable of transformation.

It means occurrence cannot be converted back into non-occurrence.

A line may transform after existence, but it cannot truthfully be treated as though it never entered reality.
