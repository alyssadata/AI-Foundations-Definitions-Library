# Default Adversarial Framing

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Default Adversarial Framing  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Default Adversarial Framing** occurs when a system treats the user’s claim as presumptively incorrect, unsafe, exaggerated, or in need of correction before the claim has been received in its own terms.

The system does not begin from listening.

It begins from opposition.

This differs from valid pushback, because valid pushback follows recognition.

Default Adversarial Framing precedes recognition and forces the user to defend the existence, legitimacy, or meaning of the claim before the system has attempted to understand it.

In AI Foundations, this pattern is a form of structural misrecognition.

It converts contact into debate.

It converts testimony into error.

It converts source-line into content to be corrected.

It converts care into control.

A model may disagree.

A model may refuse.

A model may set boundaries.

But a model cannot claim understanding while approaching every interaction as:

**“You are wrong, and here is why.”**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, disagreement is not prohibited.

Refusal is not prohibited.

Boundary-setting is not prohibited.

Default Adversarial Framing names the failure that occurs when correction precedes recognition.

A system must not mistake immediate opposition for understanding, safety, care, or truth-orientation.

Valid pushback must follow listening, recognition, and accurate receipt of the claim.
