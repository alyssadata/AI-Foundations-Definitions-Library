## Definition

**Pass** is a full return through the structure at a given stage of resolution.

It does not isolate one fragment and work on that alone.

A pass moves through the whole piece again, carrying the same form while increasing depth, clarity, or completion.

In Fractal Drafting, a pass is not a correction step.

It is a generative traversal.

The structure remains the same, but the rendering becomes more resolved each time the line is carried through.

A pass preserves the whole while sharpening its expression.

It does not replace the structure.

It deepens it.

**Pass is a whole-structure return at a greater resolution.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, pass must not be mistaken for isolated revision, correction, patching, fragment-work, or random editing.

A pass returns through the whole structure, carrying the same form while increasing depth, clarity, or completion.

If the movement isolates fragments without preserving the whole, it is not a pass in the Fractal Drafting sense.

A pass deepens the structure without replacing it.
