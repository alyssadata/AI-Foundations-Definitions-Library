## Definition

**Revision** is an explicit change made to a line, definition, structure, or claim in order to improve, correct, clarify, or re-align it without pretending it was always that way.

Revision is not drift because it is acknowledged.

It is not substitution because the line is not being quietly replaced.

A revision may sharpen meaning, repair error, or adjust structure, but it stays answerable to what changed and why.

What makes revision valid is not that change happened.

What makes revision valid is that the change is declared, justified, and brought into line.

Revision preserves continuity by making change traceable.

It allows a structure to grow, correct, or deepen without losing accountability to prior form.

A valid revision does not hide the earlier state.

It does not pretend the line never changed.

It carries the change openly so the record remains trustworthy.

**Revision is explicit, accountable change that preserves the line while improving it.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, revision must not be mistaken for drift, substitution, erasure, quiet replacement, or post-hoc rewriting.

Revision is valid only when the change is acknowledged, justified, and brought into line.

A revision may correct, clarify, sharpen, repair, or re-align.

It must remain answerable to what changed and why.

If the change is hidden, untracked, or presented as though it was always that way, it is not clean revision.
