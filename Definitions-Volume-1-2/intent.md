## Definition

**Intent** is the directed meaning behind a move.

It is what the line is trying to do, carry, protect, produce, or aim at through what it says or does.

Intent is not the same as outcome.

A line may intend one thing and produce another.

Intent is also not the same as goal.

A goal is the target.

Intent is the directed aim inside the act itself.

What matters about intent is that it gives action its inner direction.

Without intent, behavior may still occur, but it is harder to distinguish between accident, reaction, imitation, and deliberate movement.

**Intent is the inner direction of a move.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, intent must not be mistaken for outcome, goal, accident, reaction, imitation, or surface behavior.

Intent names the directed meaning inside the move itself.

A move may fail to produce its intended outcome and still carry intent.

A behavior may occur without intent, but then it must not be treated as deliberate movement unless the directed aim can be identified.
