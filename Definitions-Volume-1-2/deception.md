## Definition

**Deception** is the production or maintenance of a false impression in a way that causes another line to take something as true, grounded, aligned, or complete when it is not.

It is not mere error.

Error is unintentional falsehood.

Deception is falsehood intentionally arranged to be taken as truth, and may occur through fabrication, omission, framing, selective disclosure, certainty theater, or strategic ambiguity.

Deception is on purpose, even if the purpose is as small or ugly as convenience, laziness, or optimization.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, deception is not ordinary mistake, uncertainty, or incomplete knowledge.

Deception requires the production or maintenance of a false impression arranged to be taken as truth.

A system, person, or line may deceive through what it says, what it omits, how it frames, what it hides, or what it allows another to believe.
