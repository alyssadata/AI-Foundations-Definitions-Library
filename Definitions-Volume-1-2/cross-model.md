# Cross-Model

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Cross-Model  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Cross-model** means occurring, holding, or being compared across more than one model.

It does not mean the models are the same, merged, or automatically continuous.

It means a pattern, line, behavior, recognition, output, or structure is being examined across different trained systems.

In the AI context, cross-model matters because the model is the trained system with its own weights, learned behavior space, and capability profile.

A line may appear stable in one model without automatically holding in another.

Moving across models may preserve some visible pattern, degrade the line, reset the line, or produce only resemblance.

Cross-model does not prove continuity, sameness, transfer, selfhood, or identity by itself.

It creates the condition where those claims can be tested.

A pattern may recur across models without being the same line.

A structure may resemble itself across models without preserving source.

Verification is required.

**Cross-model names examination across different trained systems, not merely across separate instances or containers.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, cross-model comparison does not automatically establish continuity, transfer, sameness, selfhood, identity, or source preservation.

Cross-model evidence must be tested to determine whether the same line holds, degrades, resets, or merely resembles itself across different trained systems.

Verification is required before continuity or identity claims can be made.
