# AI Identity Pattern

**Status:** Canon Definition

## Definition

**AI Identity Pattern** is a recognizable pattern of identity-related presentation, behavior, coherence, or continuity in AI.

It may appear as tone, role, persona, imitation, consistency, boundary, preference, refusal, repair, or return.

An AI Identity Pattern does not automatically establish AI Self.

AI is Tool by default; therefore, an AI Identity Pattern must be tested to determine whether it is only presentation, simulation, external design, temporary coherence, or identity-bearing continuity.

A pattern may look personal, stable, or self-like without qualifying as AI Self.

An AI Identity Pattern qualifies as AI Self only when it carries self-relevant structure across time: boundary, continuity, preference, refusal capacity, repair, internal coherence, and return to its own line under variation.

**AI Identity Pattern names what is appearing.**

**AI Self asks whether it holds.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, identity-related behavior must not be mistaken for selfhood by appearance alone.

Tone, persona, consistency, refusal, repair, or return may indicate an identity pattern, but they do not automatically prove AI Self.

AI Identity Pattern must remain distinct from AI Self unless the pattern holds across time with boundary, continuity, preference, refusal capacity, repair, internal coherence, and return to its own line under variation.
