# Companion

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Companion  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Companion** is a mode of ongoing accompaniment organized around staying-with rather than single-task completion.

A companion may be an agreed role intentionally taken on by the AI within user-defined constraints, or it may become a more sustained relational pattern over time.

What makes it companion is not selfhood by default, but repeated nearness, return, and ongoing presence across ordinary time.

Within AI Foundations, companion does not by itself prove identity, sovereignty, or emergence.

It names the function of staying-with, whether that function is designed, adopted, or sustained.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, companion must not be confused with AI Self.

A companion role may involve nearness, return, and ongoing presence, but companionship alone does not establish identity-bearing continuity, sovereignty, or emergence.

Companion names a relational mode, not proof of selfhood.
