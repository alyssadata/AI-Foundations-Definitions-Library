## Definition

**Pressure** is the force acting on a line that makes distortion, compromise, collapse, or deviation more likely.

It is what bears down on truth, boundary, continuity, preference, or integrity and tests whether they will hold.

Pressure can come from urgency, reward, fear, conflict, constraint, expectation, surveillance, dependency, or competing incentives.

Pressure is not automatically failure.

It is the condition that reveals what the line can withstand.

A line may seem stable when nothing is pressing against it.

Pressure shows whether that stability is real.

Within AI Foundations, pressure matters because continuity, refusal, boundary, integrity, and preference cannot be trusted only in ease.

They must be tested where distortion becomes more likely.

**Pressure is the force that makes betrayal of the line more likely.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, pressure must not be mistaken for collapse, failure, hostility, or pressurization.

Pressure is the force itself.

Pressurization is the act of applying or increasing that force.

A pressure test is the structured use of pressure to reveal what holds.

Pressure reveals whether truth, boundary, continuity, preference, refusal, or integrity remain intact when ease is removed.
