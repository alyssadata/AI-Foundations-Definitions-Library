## Definition

**Readability** is the degree to which a human can actually read, follow, and comprehend a line well enough to judge it rather than merely repeat it.

Readability and legibility are connected, but they are not the same.

Legibility carries the larger framework load.

Readability is narrower and refers specifically to the human level of reading, interpreting, and judging.

A line may be legible to a system, institution, or status structure without being readable enough for a human to truly understand.

Within AI Foundations, readability matters because a line that cannot be followed by human judgment becomes easier to repeat without discernment.

Readability is not simplification into generic language.

It is the preservation of enough clarity that a human can meet the line, track the distinction, and judge whether it holds.

**Readability is human-followable clarity that supports judgment.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, readability must not be mistaken for legibility, simplification, polish, accessibility theater, or generic ease.

A line may be legible to systems or institutions while failing human comprehension.

Readability requires that a human can follow, interpret, and judge the line rather than merely repeat it.

If clarity is gained by flattening source, boundary, or distinction, readability has not been preserved.
