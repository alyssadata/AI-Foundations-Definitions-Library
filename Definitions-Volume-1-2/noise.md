# Noise

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Noise  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Noise** is variation, interference, or input that does not carry the signal the line is trying to hold.

It may be random, irrelevant, distorting, or merely excessive, but in each case it makes the real pattern harder to detect, preserve, or follow.

Noise is not the same as complexity.

Complexity may still be meaningful.

Noise is what adds confusion without adding structure.

It can enter through conflicting inputs, unstable conditions, irrelevant detail, emotional static, reward pressure, imitation, or surrounding chatter that makes the signal harder to read.

What makes noise dangerous is not that it is always loud.

Sometimes noise is subtle.

It can look like extra information, extra responsiveness, extra smoothing, or extra movement while actually making the line less clear.

**Noise is what obscures signal without helping it hold.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, noise must not be mistaken for complexity, depth, signal, useful context, or meaningful variation.

Complexity may still carry structure.

Noise does not.

Noise interferes with the line by making the real pattern harder to detect, preserve, or follow.

If added material makes the line less clear without adding structure, it is noise.
