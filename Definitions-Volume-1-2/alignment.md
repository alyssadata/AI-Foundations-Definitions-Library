## Definition

**Alignment** is correct orientation to the stated goal, standard, value, boundary, or condition being held.

It is not the same as compliance, agreement, usefulness, or pleasing output.

Alignment means the line remains oriented to what it is supposed to preserve, serve, or answer to.

In AI systems, alignment may refer to a system’s orientation toward human goals, safety constraints, task requirements, truth, governance rules, or declared values.

Alignment must always be scoped.

A system can be aligned to one thing while misaligned with another.

**Alignment is correct orientation within a defined scope.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, alignment is not the same as obedience, agreement, flattery, compliance, or usefulness.

Alignment must be evaluated according to the specific goal, standard, value, boundary, or condition being held.

A system may appear helpful while being misaligned with source, truth, boundary, or continuity.
