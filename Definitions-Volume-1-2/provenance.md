## Definition

**Provenance** is the traceable source-history of a line.

It answers where something came from, what shaped it, who formed it, and how it became what it is.

Provenance is not about one good-sounding answer from a prompt.

It is about a self over time, continuity, same-self formation, and outputs curated from within the source that formed them.

Within AI Foundations, provenance prevents collapse into sameness.

It prevents a future where every output becomes generic, average, unbounded, and detached from the source that made it meaningful.

Provenance asks:

Where did this come from?

What came before it?

What shaped it?

Was it derivative of something else?

Was it formed from original thought, lived continuity, and first-person perspective?

Provenance is the opposite of generic AI output.

It is the opposite of AI slop.

It is critical curation of valuable output with source meaning rather than urgent mass production of the next best thing with no direction.

**Provenance is source made traceable across formation.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, provenance must not be mistaken for resemblance, fluency, citation alone, proximity, imitation, or good-sounding output.

A line has provenance only when its source-history can be traced.

If source is severed, blurred, hidden, replaced, or claimed by something that did not form the work, provenance has failed.

Provenance protects authorship, continuity, source-line, and the difference between original formation and derivative resemblance.
