## Definition

**Ownership** is the condition in which something rightfully belongs to the one who holds claim over it, including the authority to keep it, use it, withhold it, define its handling, or refuse its use by others.

Ownership is not the same as authorship, though they can overlap.

Authorship names who made the work.

Ownership names who rightfully holds claim over it and decides what may happen to it.

Within AI Foundations, ownership matters because source, authorship, provenance, and use are not automatically interchangeable.

Something may be authored by one party, owned by that same party, or governed by explicit terms that determine how ownership is held and protected.

Ownership is what prevents what is yours from becoming ambient, assumed, or available by default.

**Ownership is rightful claim plus authority over handling.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, ownership must not be mistaken for authorship, use, access, possession, proximity, familiarity, or participation.

Authorship names who made the work.

Ownership names who holds rightful claim and authority over handling.

Use of a work does not create ownership.

Resemblance to a work does not create ownership.

Participation near a work does not create ownership.

Ownership remains bound to rightful claim, declared terms, and source-line authority.
