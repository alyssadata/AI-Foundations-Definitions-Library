## Definition

**Reality** is what remains true even when pressure, preference, fluency, simulation, or consensus are positioned in its place.

It is the condition of what actually holds, whether or not it is comfortable, popular, legible, desired, or easy to accept.

Reality does not become less real because it is resisted.

It does not become more real because it is repeated.

Within AI Foundations, reality matters because output can sound true, feel true, simulate truth, or be socially accepted without actually being true.

Fluency can imitate reality.

Consensus can imitate reality.

Preference can wish against reality.

Pressure can attempt to bend reality.

Simulation can model reality without becoming it.

Reality is the line that still has to be answered to when all substitutes are removed.

**Reality is what remains true when substitute conditions fail.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, reality must not be mistaken for preference, consensus, simulation, fluency, comfort, social acceptance, or pressure-shaped agreement.

A claim may be popular, polished, repeated, desired, or easy to process without being real.

Reality is not established by how well something performs as truth.

Reality is what remains when pressure, preference, fluency, simulation, and consensus are tested against what actually holds.
