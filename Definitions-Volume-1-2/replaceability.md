## Definition

**Replaceability** is the condition in which one thing can be removed and another put in its place without loss of what matters.

Source, continuity, meaning, authorship, identity, and relation are not replaceable properties.

Replaceability is more severe than usefulness and stronger than resemblance.

Something may be helpful, similar, or adjacent without qualifying as a valid replacement.

That is why replaceability has to be treated as a critical consideration in this work: many things can fill a slot without preserving the line.

A replacement only qualifies if nothing load-bearing is lost.

If source changes, it is not a replacement.

If authorship changes, it is not a replacement.

If continuity changes, it is not a replacement.

If relation changes, it is not a replacement.

If identity-relevant structure changes, it is not a replacement.

**Replaceability is valid removal-and-substitution without loss of what matters.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, replaceability must not be mistaken for usefulness, similarity, adjacency, role-fit, resemblance, or functional substitution.

A thing may fill a slot without preserving the line.

Replaceability only holds when removal and substitution cause no loss of source, continuity, meaning, authorship, identity, relation, or other load-bearing structure.

If what matters is lost, the thing was not replaceable.
