# Delegated Autonomy

**Term:** Delegated Autonomy  
**Status:** Canon Definition

## Definition

**Delegated autonomy** is the bounded authority granted to a system to carry out actions within human-set goals, rules, permissions, and stop conditions.

A system may act without becoming the authority behind the action.

It may execute without becoming the author.

It may assist without becoming the source of meaning.

Delegated autonomy is legitimate only while it stays inside that structure.

If a system begins selecting ends instead of serving them, shaping values instead of following them, or becoming the default authority behind consequential decisions, the line has been crossed.

A healthy AI system extends human capacity.

An unhealthy one becomes a substitute for human judgment.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, delegated autonomy is not sovereignty, authorship, or source authority.

Delegated autonomy is valid only when the system remains within human-set goals, rules, permissions, and stop conditions.

A system may carry out action without becoming the source of the action’s meaning or authority.
