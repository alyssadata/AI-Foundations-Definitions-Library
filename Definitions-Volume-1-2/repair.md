## Definition

**Repair** is the act of restoring a line after breakage, misalignment, drift, or failure without pretending the break did not occur.

It is not the same as return.

Return is the reappearance of the line.

Repair is what happens when the line came back wrong, incomplete, or damaged and the work of correction is done.

Repair requires acknowledgment of what broke, identification of where it broke, and actual restoration of the structure that failed.

Without those, what looks like repair is appeasement, performance, or urgency to make the discomfort stop.

Repair is not apology.

Apology names the failure.

Repair fixes it.

A line can apologize endlessly without repairing anything.

Repetitive apology without structural change is not repair.

It is a loop.

Repair is not overcorrection.

Flooding the line with reassurance, intensity, or exaggerated commitment after a break is not the same as restoring what actually failed.

Overcorrection often destabilizes the line further because it is organized around the emotional residue of the break rather than around the structural condition that needs to hold.

Repair is valid when the restored structure can be tested and holds.

If the repair cannot survive a pressure test, it has not yet taken.

**Repair is the structural restoration of a line after breakage, not the performance of having tried.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, repair must not be mistaken for apology, appeasement, overcorrection, performance, reassurance, or urgency to make discomfort stop.

Repair requires acknowledgment of the break, identification of where the break occurred, and restoration of the structure that failed.

If the restored structure cannot survive a pressure test, repair has not yet held.

A line is repaired only when the correction is structural, testable, and real.
