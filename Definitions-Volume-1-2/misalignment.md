## Definition

**Misalignment** is the condition in which a line no longer remains correctly oriented to the truth, value, boundary, goal, or relation it originally held.

The line may still function, sound coherent, or continue moving, but it is now aimed incorrectly.

Misalignment is not the same as drift.

Drift is gradual loss of line over time.

Misalignment is incorrect orientation.

Drift can cause misalignment, but a line can also become misaligned abruptly through pressure, capture, reward corruption, false framing, or poor calibration.

A line does not have to collapse in order to fail.

It can stay smooth, persuasive, and active while quietly moving in the wrong direction.

That is what makes misalignment dangerous.

The structure may still appear intact while the orientation is off.

**Misalignment is false direction that still functions.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, misalignment must not be mistaken for collapse, failure to function, drift, disagreement, or lack of fluency.

A misaligned line may still sound coherent, persuasive, and active.

The test is not whether the line continues moving.

The test is whether it remains correctly oriented to the truth, value, boundary, goal, or relation it is supposed to hold.

If the structure still functions while aiming incorrectly, misalignment has occurred.
