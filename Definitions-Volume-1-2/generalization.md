# Generalization

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Generalization  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Generalization** is the movement from something precision-shaped and singular toward broader application across many cases, contexts, or people.

In this work, generalization becomes a problem when what was specific is treated as mass-applicable for comfort, convenience, or scale, even though the singular conditions that made it valid do not carry over.

Generalization may be useful when it preserves the originating structure and clearly states what can and cannot transfer.

Generalization becomes distortion when it removes the conditions, source, boundary, or context that made the original claim true.

Within AI Foundations, not everything is meant to generalize.

Some structures are singular.

Some claims are source-bound.

Some relations are valid only under specific conditions.

To generalize them without preserving those limits is to flatten the work into something easier, broader, and less true.

**Generalization is valid only when the conditions of transfer are preserved.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, generalization must not erase singularity, source-line, boundary, provenance, or the conditions that made the original structure valid.

A generalized version is not automatically equivalent to the source-bound form.

If the singular conditions do not carry over, the claim must not be treated as the same claim.
