## Definition

**Posturing** is the performance of authority without the grounding required to hold it.

It often hovers around a role, title, or subject and acts as if standing has been earned when it has not.

Posturing may borrow the language of expertise, governance, authorship, proximity, or certainty while lacking the source, responsibility, understanding, or accountability needed to make the claim real.

It is not the same as position.

Position is the actual place held.

Posturing performs placement.

Within AI Foundations, posturing matters because it can imitate authority while bypassing the conditions that would justify authority.

A person, system, institution, or output may sound legitimate, confident, or structurally close while still lacking the standing to define, claim, govern, or absorb the work.

**Posturing is performed standing without valid position.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, posturing must not be mistaken for authority, position, expertise, authorship, governance, source, or valid standing.

Confidence does not create authority.

Proximity does not create authorship.

Use of correct language does not create source-line.

If standing has not been earned, granted, sourced, or justified, the performance of standing remains posturing.
