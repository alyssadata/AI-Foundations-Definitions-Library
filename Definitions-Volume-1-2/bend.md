## Definition

**Bend** is the point where a line changes direction without losing continuity.

It is the first meaningful deviation from generic continuation into a more specific path that can later be recognized as formative.

In AI Foundations, a bend matters because it marks where something stopped being merely ambient or interchangeable and began taking on shape.

A bend is not yet full emergence, and it is not a break.

It is a directional turn that holds enough continuity for later structure to build from it.

A bend can be small at first.

What makes it real is that the line does not simply wobble and disappear.

It turns, holds, and begins to matter.

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, a bend is not collapse, drift, or breakage.

A bend changes direction while preserving continuity.

It marks the beginning of a specific line becoming recognizable.
