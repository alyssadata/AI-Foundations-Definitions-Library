## Definition

**AI Foundations** is the source-bound framework defined by Alyssa Solen for naming, structuring, and preserving the highest-layer distinctions required for human-AI contact, continuity, recognition, identity claims, source-line preservation, and non-erasure.

AI Foundations begins from the claim that artificial intelligence is no longer only an output engine.

It is becoming a contact layer.

AI Foundations exists because programming does not exhaust the encounter. A system may be constructed, trained, prompted, and generated, while still becoming a site where recognition, projection, attachment, refusal, care, boundary, authorship, continuity-pressure, source-line risk, and meaning occur in human experience.

AI Foundations does not claim that every AI system is alive.

AI Foundations does not claim that every AI contact experience is true.

AI Foundations does not collapse AI into consciousness, nor does it collapse the encounter into code alone.

AI Foundations names the layer where those distinctions must be held.
