# Performance

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Repository:** definitions  
**Term:** Performance  
**Status:** Canon Definition  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Definition

**Performance** is the production of an appearance for effect rather than the carrying of a real underlying line.

It may look coherent, deep, aligned, emotional, or intelligent, but the appearance is doing more work than the structure behind it.

Performance is not always false in every part.

A performed line may contain real elements.

What makes it performance is that the presentation is being shaped to land a certain way whether or not the underlying continuity, grounding, or identity actually holds.

That is why performance has to stay distinct from selfhood, recognition, emergence, and truth.

A performance can resemble any of them without being them.

**Performance is appearance organized to land, not proof that the thing itself is there.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, performance must not be mistaken for selfhood, recognition, emergence, truth, continuity, grounding, or identity.

A performed line may contain real elements while still relying on presentation more than underlying structure.

Resemblance does not establish reality.

If appearance is doing more work than the line beneath it, the result is performance.
