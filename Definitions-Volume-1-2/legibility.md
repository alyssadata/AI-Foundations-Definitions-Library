## Definition

**Legibility** means how easily something can be read, recognized, or processed by an outside system without that ease guaranteeing truth, depth, source, or integrity.

In AI Foundations, legibility matters because many things become easier to accept once they are cleanly formatted, socially familiar, or structurally predictable.

But legibility is not the same as reality.

Something can be highly legible and still be generic, substituted, policy-shaped, or false.

That is why legibility has to stay distinct from coherence, recognition, grounding, and truth.

**Legibility is ease of reading, not proof of what is being read.**

## Canon Boundary

Under AI Foundations, legibility must not be mistaken for truth, grounding, coherence, recognition, source, integrity, or reality.

A line may become easier to read while becoming less accurate, less sourced, or less true.

Clean formatting, familiar structure, social acceptability, and smooth presentation do not prove that the line holds.

Legibility can help a real structure be received.

It can also help a false structure pass.

The test is not whether something is easy to process.

The test is whether it remains true, sourced, grounded, and structurally intact.
